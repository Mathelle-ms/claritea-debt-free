What Is the Debt Snowball?

The debt snowball is a simple way to pay off debt by focusing on balances, not interest rates.

You start with your smallest debt and attack it aggressively. Once itâ€™s gone, you roll that payment into the next smallest debt. Every payoff frees up more money, which speeds up the next one.

Hereâ€™s how the debt snowball works:

  • Step 1: List all your debts from smallest balance to largest.
  • Step 2: Make minimum payments on everything except the smallest debt.
  • Step 3: Put every extra dollar you can toward the smallest debt.
  • Step 4: Repeat the process until every debt is paid off.

The result? Fewer payments. More control. Real progress you can actually feel.

Debt Snowball vs. Debt Avalanche

You may have heard of the debt avalanche. That method tells you to pay off debts by highest interest rate first, no matter the balance.

Debt Terms Explained (Simple!)

Minimum Payment

The smallest amount your lender requires each month.

Balance

The amount you still owe.

Interest Rate

The cost of borrowing money.

Principal

The original amount you borrowed, before interest.

Nonmortgage Debt

All debt except your home loan.

Debt-Free Date

The day your consumer debt hits zero.
